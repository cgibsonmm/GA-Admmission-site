About Me

Hello, I'm Corey Gibson. After spending my childhood living in on the East Coast of Florida, I moved to Upstate New York to pursue a degree in film. In high school, T.V. Production, playing guitar and surfing consumed most of my life, I spent most high school days hiding behind an old Casablanca video editing system. Then after school, I would head to the Cocoa Beach Pier and surf until dark or later. After graduation and moving to New York, I became consumed by work. A short time after, my visions of a career in the film industry turned to become a vision of a professional FireFighter and Paramedic. I joined my local volunteer fire department and started taking any class I could get my hands on. In less than a handful of months, my life went from shooting videos and surfing to working 12 plus hours a day on an ambulance and a lieutenant at The Hurleyville Fire Dept.

Skip forward a year or two and while working on an ambulance with a partner that was a self-proclaimed "hacker," I started to take an interest in programming and to learn a more in-depth knowledge of computers. Circa 2009 I built my first word-press website it was called EMS-NOW.com and had hopes of it becoming a Reddit style blog site where emergency workers and first responders would be able to share their personal stories and experiences while on "the job." At the time, I used Wordpress tools to create this site, but with my lack of real knowledge and the cost of different hosting tools, the website did not last for more than a few months. While I built this first site and it failed miserably, still I was fascinated with the idea of creating different websites, and the knowable programming could offer.

Once again, I put my dreams to the wayside when I took a full-time position in 2014 as an EMT for FDNY and moved to Brooklyn with the hope of shortly after that being able to be promoted to Fire Fighter. Since then, I have been working overnights on an ambulance in Williamsburg. This work is hard tiring and trying and offers little room for advancement. About three years ago I tried my hand at building a dating website for the local community in Sullivan County New York, which is still live today at sullivandates and was built using techniques learned in The Ruby On Rails Tutorial by Michael Hartl. While I found this book to be insightful after reading and re-reading I felt as though I did not have the skills needed to build a full site and the site still sits in the state of a "coming soon" landing page. Since then, I have been using different resources online to learn in and out pieces of Website development and software design. From Codecadamy, Team Treehouse, and Launch School.

Today is and I am 29 years old living in Queens with my girlfriend, my dog, and a cat. Over the past few years, we have had the opportunity to travel often. I have driven The Pacific Coast Highway, witnessed Old Faithful, visited The Louver, and attend Oktoberfest in Munich. In total, I believe I have been to 22 different country's and. I am also an avid backpacker and camper, and a PADI certified Scuba diver and have dove all over the Caribbean.
